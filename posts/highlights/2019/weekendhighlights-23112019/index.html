Weekend Highlights: 23 Nov 2019

Posted: by aoanla 2019-11-18 19:20 [Source]
Tags:  argentine roller derby australian roller derby belgian roller derby british roller derby danish roller derby emirates roller derby french roller derby german roller derby italian roller derby japanese roller derby national tournament new zealand roller derby norwegian roller derby polish roller derby refereeing clinic roller derby sevens spanish roller derby weekend highlights

You might think things would slow down in derby in late November, but this is still an exceptionally busy week, worldwide. There's multiple tournaments and international bouts: returning tournaments in Belgium, with Skate Around #3; in Italy, with Scusa Mamma, Ma Sono Una Derby Girl; and in Argentina, with Juguemos Pué; in the UK, returing Roller Derby Sevens and Sur5al events; and continuing tournament series in Germany, France, and Japan (where Derby Nights 4x4's fixture is extra long due to the postponed bout in October).

The rules are, as usual: highlights limited to 1 event per country, with an "extra" event allowed for a different kind of fixture (so, 1 tournament and 1 bootcamp), or if they involve Scottish leagues (since we are the Scottish Roller Derby Blog). Other notability might also allow the extra event - great posters, notable teams, etc. (League birthdays may count as "special" enough, at our discretion ;) )

In a bid to make this list as useful as possible, we've avoided links to Facebook except where noted. (Links to Teams are to non-Facebook resources - Instagram, or actual team pages - we'd strongly recommend that Teams get themselves an actual webpage [we can help host one if you need help]).

Locations are roughly organised East-West (with things before the weekend out sequence at the start).

[FACEBOOK LINK]

Kingston City Rollers host a special event: Roll for Respect. This free afternoon serves as both an introduction to roller derby, and a chance to see KCR play an exhibition bout themselves... but it also supports the Kingston City Council "16 Days of Activism Against Gender-Base Violence" campaign, which is why it makes our highlights.

[FACEBOOK LINK (flyer)]

The Derby Nights 4x4 tournament continues, with a specially busy fixture due to the postponement of the October bouts due to extreme weather in the Tokyo metropolitan area. The Spiral Cats have reported this event as hosting a "Charity Game", but as yet we do not have any further details on this.

Venue: Tokyo Dome Roller Skate Arena, 1 Chome-3-６１号 Koraku, Bunkyo City, Tokyo, Japan

[FACEBOOK LINK] [FTS LINK 1] [FTS LINK 2]

Also of interest, due to their attendees:

Fri-Sun: The Internet

[FACEBOOK LINK]

As we trailed a bit ago, Rule 56 , the UK based Roller Derby community coaching entity, is holding their 2019 Coaching Summit, Derby Stance Online, over this weekend. With a host of exciting speakers, all available via a webinar system (which allows interactive sessions for attendees), and a live machine-translated transcription service, to support those who don't speak English, or are deaf or hard of hearing, this is planned to be Rule 56's best edition of the event. Read our linked trailer article for more details, or follow the Rule 56 link for their own direct resources.

Event starts: 0830 PM (GMT) Thursday (Keynote); 1130 AM GMT Friday (main conference)

Venue: The Internet, http://www.rulefiftysix.com/derby-stance
